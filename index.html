Twin-Wall Polypropylene: The Architecture of Performance

Twin-wall (also called fluted or corrugated) polypropylene sheet is constructed from two parallel PP skins connected by internal fluted channels running the length of the sheet. This geometry gives PP corrugated an exceptional stiffness-to-weight ratio — substantially stiffer per unit mass than solid sheet of the same total thickness. The result is a material that handles structural packaging loads while remaining lightweight enough to reduce freight costs and improve handling ergonomics.

The closed-cell construction of twin-wall PP provides inherent resistance to moisture. Unlike paper-based corrugated materials, PP twin-wall does not absorb water, does not delaminate when wet, and does not lose structural integrity when exposed to condensation, humidity, or direct liquid contact. For refrigerated, produce, or outdoor applications where moisture is unavoidable, that resistance is not optional — it is the entire reason PP corrugated exists.

Reusability is the other defining advantage. A properly handled PP corrugated box or slip sheet can complete 10 to 30 or more use cycles under typical conditions, compared to a single use for most paper corrugated alternatives. Over the lifespan of a packaging program, that reuse factor compresses per-trip packaging cost dramatically and reduces the volume of packaging waste generated per unit of product shipped.

Food-Grade Compliance and FSMA Readiness

Elipacko's PP corrugated products designated for food-contact use are manufactured using polypropylene resin grades that comply with FDA 21 CFR 177.1520, the federal regulation governing olefin polymers for food-contact articles. This ensures that our PP corrugated sheets, boxes, and slip sheets used in direct or indirect food-contact applications meet the extractable thresholds and additive restrictions required for food safety compliance in the United States.

The Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA) imposes preventive control requirements on food facilities, and packaging material compliance is a component of those controls. Elipacko supports customer FSMA compliance programs by providing material certificates, resin data sheets, and FDA compliance letters for food-contact PP corrugated products. Our documentation package is designed to support third-party audits under SQF, BRC, and GFSI schemes.

Polypropylene's chemical inertness means it does not impart flavor, odor, or extractable contaminants to food products under normal use conditions. This property, combined with the smooth, cleanable surface of PP corrugated, makes it a practical choice for any food-chain application where packaging hygiene and compliance documentation are mandatory requirements.
